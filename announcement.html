By Ardie Cabatin | October 18, 2024


    In a show of gratitude and preparation for their upcoming international performance, the CLSU Bithay Sining Dance Company recently paid a courtesy visit to Dr. Florante P. Ibarra, Head of the Cultural Affairs Office and Dean of the College of Education at Central Luzon State University. The CLSU Bithay Sining Dance Company together with Associate Professor Adonis Voltaire M. Villanueva will represent the Philippines in the prestigious International Cultural & Arts Festival (ICAF), to be held at Sungai Melaka, Malaysia, from October 21-28, 2024. This international event is set to gather performers from various cultures across the globe, giving the CLSU Bithay Sining Dance Company the opportunity to showcase the richness of Filipino heritage through dance. During the visit, Dr. Ibarra expressed his pride in the groupâ€™s achievements and emphasized the importance of cultural preservation and global representation. He encouraged the dancers to bring not only their best performances but also the spirit of Filipino artistry to an international audience. He also acknowledged the hard work and dedication of the group in preparing for such a prestigious event, underscoring the significance of promoting Filipino culture on a global stage. The Bithay Sining Dance Company, well-known for their dynamic and authentic renditions of traditional Filipino dances, are honored to take part in ICAF, carrying with them the pride of CLSU and the Philippines. With their passion and artistry, they aim to leave a lasting impression and further the appreciation of Philippine culture among international audiences. This visit was a meaningful send-off, solidifying the universityâ€™s support for its cultural ambassadors as they embark on this exciting and important international journey

Join Zumba Session Hosted by the Office of the Vice President for Academic Affairs


By Ardie Cabatin | February 20, 2024


    In a spirited display of unity and wellness, the Cultural Affairs Office (CAO) Performers actively participated in the Zumba session organized by the Office of the Vice President for Academic Affairs (OVPAA) on February 19, 2025, at the OVPAA Grounds in front of the CASS Pavilion. The event, aimed at promoting physical fitness and mental well-being, gathered faculty, staff, and students for a high-energy, dance-fueled workout. Members of the CAO Performers enthusiastically joined the session, showcasing their commitment not only to cultural excellence but also to holistic well-being. The energy of the participants created a lively and positive atmosphere, reflecting the CLSU community's dedication to health and camaraderie. The Zumba session is part of the university's broader wellness program, which seeks to encourage healthier lifestyles and foster a supportive community environment. Through such initiatives, CLSU continues to promote not only academic and cultural excellence but also the overall well-being of its members.
